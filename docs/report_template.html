    148 tracks from my library · positives = saved songs · weak negatives = unsaved Made-For-You mix tracks (a PU-learning setup)

    Looked brilliant. Was fraud: a source-count feature encoded the label's origin, and artist liked-rates were computed across CV folds.
    v3 · Honest

    Content features only, whole artists held out. The model must generalize taste to artists it has never seen — and still beats chance.

    On 148 rows, an AUC of 0.95 is an accusation, not a result.

    Favorite failure mode. A song literally titled "A COLD PLAY" ranked fourth in Part I — the char-ngram TF-IDF learned that strings spelled like coldplay are things I like, and it isn't even wrong about why. Title text is a real signal and a real trap.

    Part II — the discovery engine

    Finding undervalued songs adds two problems on top of taste: the model must score tracks from a different distribution than it trained on, and "you'll love this" claims need uncertainty attached. The design — stress-tested by a second model (GPT-5 Codex), which rejected fancier options (Elkan–Noto calibration, conformal sets, KDE novelty) as unstable at n=148:

    ComponentChoiceWhy not fancier
    PreferenceRegularized LR, cross-validated percentile scoreElkan–Noto "calibrated P(love)" needs labeling assumptions this data violates
    Uncertainty300 grouped-artist bootstrap refits → stability bandsConformal sets would burn half of 148 rows on calibration
    ObscurityDeezer popularity rank → log-percentile (never a model feature)Popularity inside the model would launder the bias we're fighting
    Rankingpreference × obscurityγ, per-artist cap of 2UCB bonus rewards being weird, not being good, in a one-shot setting

    Honesty checks, all leave-one-artist-out: combined features (AUC 0.606) beat metadata-only (0.544) and text-only (0.550); positive lift of 1.3× in the top quintile; and the top-15 gem list was re-derived under γ ∈ {0.5, 1, 2} — three-way Jaccard overlap 0.43, reported rather than hidden, which is why the γ dial is exposed in the UI instead of baked in.

    What I'd tell a reviewer

    • Scores are percentiles, not probabilities. With 51 positives and behaviorally-selected weak negatives, calibrated "P(love)" claims would be false precision. Bands are ranking stability, not confidence intervals.
    • The pool is authored. 321 candidates come from 16 taste-adjacent artist searches on Deezer; gems outside those neighborhoods are invisible. Fixing that means candidate generation from co-listening graphs — the natural v3.
    • Era is imputed at artist level for candidates (Deezer search returns no dates); the library uses real years.
    • The next label is a listen. The exploration dial is round one of an active-learning loop: every play/skip on a crate pick is a true label that retrains the engine — that closed loop is the actual product.
